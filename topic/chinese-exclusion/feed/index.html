The slogan, popularized by infamous California nativist and labor leader Denis Kearney (1847-1907), was a central thematic of the anti-Chinese propaganda that helped fuel the Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882.

As with this cap pistol, the slogan was often accompanied by iconography depicting a Chinese man with a queue, being symbolically (and literally) chased or beaten from the United States.

From the Yoshio Kishi and Irene Yah-Ling Sun Collection; Fales Library and Special Collections, New York University Libraries.
