Project http://jonoyuan.github.io/yellowperilarchive an Archive of anti-Asian fear Tue, 05 Apr 2016 20:47:06 +0000 en-US hourly 1 https://wordpress.org/?v=5.0.8 Yellow Peril and The Vietnam War http://jonoyuan.github.io/yellowperilarchive/commentary/210/ http://jonoyuan.github.io/yellowperilarchive/commentary/210/#respond Sun, 03 Aug 2014 16:15:34 +0000 http://jonoyuan.github.io/yellowperilarchive/?p=210 Continue reading Yellow Peril and The Vietnam War →]]> 50 years after the Gulf of Tonkin resolution created the legal justification for American military involvement in Vietnam, John Kuo Wei Tchen reflects on how “Orientalphobia,” made legible in part through films like Manchurian Candidate, created a climate of fear and paranoia in which misinformation and militarism could thrive.

“In short, the larger racial culture predisposed voters to believe a yellow-peril war script. And the same culture had true believers imagining that the virile Marines would quickly prevail over short, heathen “gooks.” In hindsight, we know none of this was true at that time. We should also know that this paranoid style has not served our nation well in the half century since (WMDs, anyone?). Putting the Gulf of Tonkin “incident” into this larger historical arc helps us understand why, when stereotypes pervade the public imagination, a democratic society can be vulnerable to systemic misinformation.”
